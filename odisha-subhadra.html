Odisha’s Subhadra Scheme: CSCs Paving the Way for Women's Empowerment
Aiming to enroll over 1 crore women, CSCs play a crucial role in assisting eligible beneficiaries under the Subhadra Scheme.
By CSC VLE

The Odisha State Government has introduced the 'Subhadra Yojana' as a significant step toward women's empowerment. Under this scheme, women in Odisha, aged between 21 and 60 years, would receive financial support of ₹10,000 annually for five years, totaling ₹50,000. The first installment of ₹5,000 was distributed on September 17, coinciding with the birthday of Hon'ble Prime Minister Narendra Modi. The funds transferred directly into the beneficiaries' bank accounts via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT).

The goal of the scheme is to enroll at least 1 crore women across the state, with Common Service Centers (CSCs) being tasked with promoting awareness and helping eligible women complete the application process.

Women will be issued debit cards as part of the scheme. An incentive of ₹500 will be awarded to 100 women in each gram panchayat and urban body who make the highest number of digital transactions.

How to Apply:

To apply for the Subhadra Yojana, women can obtain the application form from their local Anganwadi center, Block office, or CSC center. After filling out the form, it should be submitted along with the required documents. There is no fee for applying to the scheme.

Eligibility Criteria:

Women who are native residents of Odisha, aged 21 to 60, with a family income not exceeding ₹2.5 lakh annually, are eligible for the Subhadra Yojana. They must also be enrolled under the National Food Security Act (NFSA) or the State Food Security Scheme (SFSS).

Women employed in government positions or with an income tax-paying family member are not eligible. Additionally, those already benefiting from other government schemes will not qualify for this program.
